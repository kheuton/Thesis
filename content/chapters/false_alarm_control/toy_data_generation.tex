We generated positive and negative examples by sampling from 2-D Gaussian distributions as described below : 

For the positive class, three blobs are created from 3 different Gaussians with means positioned at [0.7, 2.5], [0.7, 1.0], and [0.7, 0.0] and variances [0.06, 0.1], [0.1, 0.1] and [0.06, 0.06] respectively. The number of samples in these blobs are 30, 60, and 30, respectively.

Similarly, for the negative class, blobs are positioned at [2.25, 1.5], [0.0, 3.0], and [0.0, 0.5], and variances [0.1, 0.2], [0.05, 0.05] and [0.05, 0.05] with respective sample sizes of 400, 30, and 20. We intentionally sample more negative data-points than positive data-points to create a dataset with class-imbalance, which is common in health record datasets.
